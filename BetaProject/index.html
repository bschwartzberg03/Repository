The Headaches of Route One Apartments

By: Brandon Schwartzberg

Students at the University of Maryland are only guaranteed university-owned housing for their freshman year. After that, they are entirely on their own. The result is countless apartments that have been developed along Route One, the road right off campus — Aster, Terrapin Row, College Park Towers, Landmark, The Varsity, University View, Tempo and The Nine —alongside other places currently under construction — The Standard, Aspen Heights and The Hub. All three of those apartments are expected to be ready by Fall 2023.

Each apartment has certain amenities — pools, gyms, study rooms and courtyards just to name a few — that distinguish themselves from their competition. With the number of apartments available in addition to the different amenities and locations that each provide, students have plenty of options for apartments once they move off campus. But that isn't necessarily a positive.

One of the most important factors that goes into apartment hunting is the costs. Landmark is at the top of the list as one of the most expensive apartments for University of Maryland students, as the lowest four-bedroom, four-bathroom unit costs $1,609/month, and that's without any extra fees that could be thrown in. Meanwhile, University View — one of the cheaper apartments — costs $1,290/month — without extra fees — for a four-bedroom, four-bathroom unit, a big difference in comparison to Landmark. While Landmark is seen by many students as the premiere apartment option, its cost is a major element that must be taken into account.


Another important component when it comes to apartment hunting for students is the location, which varies based on certain majors. For journalism majors, Terrapin Row presents the best location as it’s the shortest walk to Knight Hall. But for computer science majors, The Varsity presents the best location with the shortest walk to the Brendan Iribe Center for Computer Science and Engineering.

"The location will be very close to campus and close to Trader Joe's for grocery shopping." — Ryan Warrenfeltz Jr."

Ryan Warrenfeltz Jr., a sophomore Environmental Science and Policy major, decided to live at one of the new apartments, The Standard, for next year. Warrenfeltz valued multiple factors during his apartment-hunting process.

"Cost was important, [and] the location will be very close to campus and close to Trader Joe's for grocery shopping," Warrenfeltz said. "The amenities and the rooms, everybody will have their own bedroom and a full-size bed which was nice."

Landmark's cost alone put it out of contention for Warrenfeltz and his roomates.


It wasn't just those factors that played a role in Warrenfeltz' decision to live at The Standard, as he thinks it will be cool to be in the inaugural group for the new apartment. The apartment-hunting process wasn't easy for Warrenfeltz, as his roommates and he spent just under a week trying to nail a selection — among the countless options — for the next school year. That left him feeling overwhelmed at times, as he feels a lot of the places "seem fairly homogeneous."

"It's hard to find a place to start, especially if you don't know anything about any of them, like having to look up each one and know where each one is," Warrenfeltz said.

Keara Bruno, a junior Journalism major, initially looked for apartments in 2021, when there were less options available. She settled on Terrapin Row, and has decided to lease there again for her senior year as in her eyes, her unit has been a good living situation. While she didn't feel very overwhelmed — given her limited choices — she worries for future College Park students.

"I think for the future with all of these apartments being built, it's definitely going to be overwhelming for people to decide where they want to live," Bruno said.
